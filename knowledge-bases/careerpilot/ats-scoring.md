**Product:** CareerPilot AI  
**Support Level:** Level 1, Level 2  
**Last Updated:** April 3, 2026

---

## What is an ATS Score?

ATS (Applicant Tracking System) score measures how well your resume will perform when processed by automated hiring software. Most companies use ATS to filter resumes before human review.

CareerPilot AI analyzes your resume against common ATS requirements and provides a compatibility score from 0-100%.

---

## Score Interpretation

### 85%+ = ATS-Friendly Layout ✅
Your resume has excellent ATS compatibility:
- Standard section headers recognized
- Clean, parseable formatting
- No problematic elements detected
- High chance of passing ATS filters

**Action:** Your resume is ready for job applications.

---

### 70-84% = Good with Minor Issues ⚠️
Your resume is mostly compatible but has small issues:
- Some sections may not parse perfectly
- Minor formatting concerns detected

**Action:** Review suggestions and make targeted improvements.

---

### 65% = Formatting Issues 🔴
Your resume has significant ATS problems:
- Text boxes detected (major issue)
- Tables used for layout
- Non-standard fonts or formatting
- Complex design elements

**Common Cause:** Text boxes and tables

**Fix:** Convert headers to plain bold text (no text boxes)

---

### Below 65% = Major Redesign Needed ❌
Your resume will likely be filtered out by ATS:
- Multiple problematic elements
- Non-parseable sections
- Image-heavy design
- Heavily formatted template

**Action:** Consider using CareerPilot's resume builder or a simple template.

## Common Issues and Fixes

### Text Boxes (Most Common Issue)

**Problem:** Text boxes look great visually but ATS cannot read content inside them.

**How to Check:**
1. In Word: Click on text - if a box border appears, it's a text box
2. In Google Docs: Text boxes show as "Drawing" objects

**Fix:**
1. Select all content in the text box
2. Copy the text (Ctrl+C / Cmd+C)
3. Delete the text box
4. Paste as plain text (Ctrl+Shift+V)
5. Format using bold/underline instead of boxes

---

### Tables for Layout

**Problem:** Tables used to create multi-column layouts confuse ATS parsers.

**Fix:**
1. Remove table structure
2. Convert to simple vertical layout
3. Use line breaks and spacing instead

---

### Graphics and Icons

**Problem:** Icons for contact info, skill bars, and graphics are invisible to ATS.

**Fix:**
1. Replace icons with text labels
2. Remove skill rating bars (use text: "Expert", "Proficient", etc.)
3. Remove decorative elements

---

### Headers and Footers

**Problem:** ATS often skips header/footer content entirely.

**Fix:**
1. Move contact information to the main body
2. Put your name at the top of the document body
3. Avoid putting important info in headers/footers

---

### Non-Standard Fonts

**Problem:** Unusual fonts may not render correctly.

**Recommended Fonts:**
- Arial
- Calibri
- Times New Roman
- Garamond
- Helvetica

**Avoid:** Script fonts, decorative fonts, custom fonts

## How to Improve Your Score

### Quick Wins (5 minutes):
1. Replace text boxes with plain formatted text
2. Remove tables
3. Change to a standard font
4. Move contact info from header to body

### Full Optimization (30 minutes):
1. Use CareerPilot's ATS-friendly resume builder
2. Choose a single-column template
3. Use standard section headers:
   - Professional Summary
   - Work Experience
   - Education
   - Skills
   - Certifications
4. Re-upload and check new score

## Score Not Updating?

If your ATS score doesn't change after making edits:

1. **Clear browser cache** - Ctrl+Shift+R (or Cmd+Shift+R on Mac)
2. **Re-upload the file** - Don't just save; upload the new version
3. **Check file format** - Ensure you're uploading .docx or text-based .pdf
4. **Wait 30 seconds** - Score calculation may take a moment

## Frequently Asked Questions

### "Why is my score low when my resume looks professional?"
Visual appeal ≠ ATS compatibility. Many beautiful templates use elements (text boxes, tables, graphics) that ATS cannot read.

### "Will a 100% score guarantee I get interviews?"
No. ATS score measures technical compatibility, not content quality. You still need:
- Relevant keywords
- Tailored content for each job
- Strong accomplishments

### "Should I have different resumes for different jobs?"
Yes. Tailoring keywords and content to each job posting improves both ATS scores and human reviewer interest.

## When to Escalate

**Escalate to Level 2 if:**
- Score is stuck at same value despite changes
- Score decreased after making recommended improvements
- Conflicting advice from the system

**Escalate to Level 3 if:**
- Suspected bug in scoring algorithm
- Multiple users report incorrect scores
